MOMO

All about momo

Nepali momo are delicious dumplings that are a staple in Nepali cuisine. hey come filled with a variety of ingredients, including spiced minced meat such as chicken, pork, or buffalo, or a vegetarian mix of finely chopped vegetables and paneer.

Typically served with a tangy dipping sauce known as achar, which is made from tomatoes, chili, and spices, momo are enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or even a main course.The combination of the tender, flavorful filling and the aromatic, slightly spicy dipping sauce makes momo a beloved treat in Nepal and beyond, often bringing people together in social gatherings or family meals.

Ingredients

For the dough

  • Flour: 2 cups
  • Water: 3-4 cups
  • Salt and oil:1 teaspoon

For the fillings

  • Ground meat (such as chicken, pork, or buffalo): 1 cup (for non-vegetarian) or paneer: 1 cup, crumbled (for vegetarian)
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 3-4 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, minced
  • Cabbage: 1 cup, finely chopped (for vegetarian)
  • Carrot: 1 medium, finely chopped or grated (for vegetarian)
  • Green chilies: 1-2, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • Coriander leaves: A small bunch, chopped
  • Salt: To taste
  • Garam masala: 1/2 teaspoon (optional, for a richer flavor)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1 tablespoon of oil (optional).
    • Gradually add approximately 3/4 cup of water, mixing continuously, until a soft dough forms.
    • Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until smooth. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • In a bowl, mix 1 cup of ground meat (or crumbled paneer for vegetarian) with 1 medium finely chopped onion, 3-4 minced garlic cloves, and 1-inch minced ginger.
    • Add 1 cup of finely chopped cabbage, 1 finely chopped or grated carrot, 1-2 finely chopped green chilies, and a small bunch of chopped coriander leaves.
    • Season with 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, salt, and black pepper to taste. Optionally, add 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder and 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala.
    • Mix all ingredients well to combine.
  3. Assemble the Momos:
    • Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle on a floured surface.
    • Spoon a small amount of filling onto the center of each circle.
    • Fold the edges of the dough over the filling to form a pleated pouch, pinching the edges to seal the momo.
  4. Cook the Momos:
    • For steaming: Place the momos in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper or cabbage leaves. Steam for about 15-20 minutes or until the dough is cooked through.
    • For frying: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the momos until golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally.
  5. Prepare the Dipping Sauce (Achar):
    • In a blender, combine 2 chopped tomatoes, 2-3 chopped green chilies, 2-3 garlic cloves, and 1-inch piece of ginger.
    • Add a handful of chopped coriander leaves, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, and salt to taste.
    • Blend until smooth. Optionally, heat 1 tablespoon of oil and temper with 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds, then mix with the blended sauce.
  6. Serve the momos hot with the achar dipping sauce.
